Ocean Fun Facts!

Dive into amazing trivia about our incredible oceans and the life they contain

Marine Creatures

The blue whale's heart is so large that a human could swim through its arteries.

A blue whale's heart can weigh up to 400 pounds (180 kg) and is about the size of a small car. Each heartbeat can be heard from 2 miles away, and it only beats 8-10 times per minute when diving.

Ocean Geography

We've explored less than 5% of Earth's oceans.

Despite covering 71% of Earth's surface, the ocean remains largely unexplored. The average depth is 12,080 feet (3,682 m), with the Mariana Trench reaching 36,201 feet (11,034 m) deep. We have better maps of Mars than of our ocean floor.

Marine Science

The ocean produces at least 50% of the planet's oxygen.

Phytoplankton, microscopic marine algae, contribute between 50-85% of the oxygen in Earth's atmosphere through photosynthesis. These tiny organisms form the base of the marine food web and are more abundant than all terrestrial plants combined.

Marine Creatures

A mantis shrimp can throw a punch at 50 mph (80 km/h).

The mantis shrimp's club-like appendages accelerate faster than a .22 caliber bullet, generating forces of over 1,500 newtons. Their strike is so fast it boils the water around it and creates flashes of light (sonoluminescence).

Ocean Geography

The Pacific Ocean is wider than the moon.

At its widest point near the equator, the Pacific Ocean stretches about 12,300 miles (19,800 km) from Indonesia to Colombia. The moon's diameter is only about 2,159 miles (3,474 km), making the Pacific more than 5 times wider.

Ocean History

The deepest shipwreck ever found is the USS Johnston at 20,406 feet (6,220 m).

Discovered in 2019 in the Philippine Sea, this World War II destroyer sank during the Battle off Samar in 1944. It took multiple expeditions with advanced submersibles to locate and document the wreck at this extreme depth.

Marine Science

There are underwater rivers and lakes on the ocean floor.

Brine pools form when salt-saturated water (4-5 times saltier than seawater) collects in depressions. These "lakes" have distinct shorelines and can even have waves. Underwater rivers form from dense sediment flows called turbidity currents.

Marine Creatures

Some corals can live for more than 4,000 years.

Deep-sea black corals found off Hawaii have been aged at 4,265 years old. These slow-growing organisms provide valuable records of ocean conditions over millennia in their growth rings, similar to tree rings.

Did You Know?

The pressure at the deepest part of the ocean is equivalent to having about 50 jumbo jets piled on top of you.

More historical artifacts are preserved in the ocean than in all the world's museums combined.

The ocean contains 20 million tons of gold dissolved in its waters - about 1 gram per 100 million metric tons of seawater.

Underwater waves (internal waves) can reach heights of 650 feet (200 m) but are invisible at the surface.
