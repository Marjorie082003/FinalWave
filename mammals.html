Deep Ocean Mammals

Sperm Whale

The deepest diving mammal, reaching depths of over 2,000 meters in search of giant squid. Their large heads contain spermaceti oil that helps with buoyancy control.

2,000m+ dives
Echolocation

Cuvier's Beaked Whale

Record holder for the deepest and longest dive of any marine mammal - reaching nearly 3,000 meters and staying submerged for over 3 hours.

2,992m record
3h 42m dive

Elephant Seal

These massive seals routinely dive to 1,500 meters, holding their breath for up to 100 minutes. They spend 80% of their lives underwater.

1,500m dives

Risso's Dolphin

Known for their scarred appearance, these dolphins dive to 600 meters to feed on deep-water squid and octopus.

Remarkable Adaptations

Pressure Resistance

Collapsible lungs, flexible rib cages, and specialized proteins protect their bodies from extreme pressure at depth.

Oxygen Management

Enhanced blood volume, high myoglobin concentrations, and reduced heart rate conserve oxygen during dives.

Thermoregulation

Thick blubber layers and specialized blood vessel arrangements maintain body heat in frigid deep waters.

Echolocation

Sophisticated sonar systems allow them to navigate and hunt in complete darkness at extreme depths.
