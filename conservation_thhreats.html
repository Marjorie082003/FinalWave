The Plastic Tide

8M tons/year 700+ species affected

Plastic waste persists for centuries, breaking into microplastics that infiltrate every marine ecosystem. By 2050, there could be more plastic than fish in the ocean by weight.

Microplastic Invasion

51T particles 100% of turtles affected

Microplastics have been found in the deepest ocean trenches and Arctic ice. They absorb toxins and are ingested by plankton, entering the entire marine food web.

Industrial Fishing

90% exploited 2.7T fish caught/year

Modern fishing fleets are 2-3 times larger than what oceans can sustainably support. Bycatch accounts for 40% of global catch, including dolphins, turtles, and seabirds.

Ecosystem Collapse

90% predators gone 60% species decline

Removing top predators disrupts entire food webs. Some fish populations have declined by 90% since 1950. Without change, all commercially fished species may collapse by 2048.

Coral Bleaching

50% reefs lost 90% by 2050

Rising sea temperatures cause corals to expel symbiotic algae, leading to bleaching. The 2016 Great Barrier Reef bleaching affected 93% of the reef system.

Sea Level Rise

3.7mm/year 187M displaced

Thermal expansion and melting ice are causing sea levels to rise, threatening coastal ecosystems and communities. Projections suggest 0.3-1m rise by 2100.

Shellfish Crisis

30% more acidic 60% species at risk

Increased CO2 absorption lowers pH, making it harder for shellfish and corals to build calcium carbonate structures. Pteropods (key food source) show shell dissolution.

Cascade Effects

50% calcifiers affected $1T ecosystem value

Acidification disrupts entire food webs. Dungeness crab larvae show developmental delays, while some fish exhibit altered behavior and predator avoidance.
