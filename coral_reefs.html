Coral Reefs

The rainforests of the sea - vibrant ecosystems teeming with life

Why Coral Reefs Matter

Coral reefs are among the most valuable ecosystems on Earth, supporting an incredible diversity of life

Biodiversity Hotspots

Though they cover less than 1% of the ocean floor, coral reefs are home to 25% of all marine species.

Coastal Protection

Reefs act as natural breakwaters, reducing wave energy by 97% and protecting shorelines from storms.

Food Security

Reefs provide food and income for over 500 million people worldwide through fisheries and tourism.

Medical Discoveries

Many marine organisms found on reefs have yielded important medicines, including treatments for cancer.

Economic Value

Coral reefs provide goods and services worth about $375 billion each year to the global economy.

Oxygen Production

Like rainforests, coral reefs produce oxygen and absorb carbon dioxide, helping regulate Earth's climate.

Spectacular Coral Species

Discover the incredible diversity of corals that build these underwater cities

Brain Coral

Diploria labyrinthiformis

Named for its grooved surface resembling a human brain, this slow-growing coral can live for 900 years.

Staghorn Coral

Acropora cervicornis

This branching coral grows rapidly (4-8 inches per year) and provides critical habitat for reef fish.

Elkhorn Coral

Acropora palmata

These large, branching corals create essential breakwaters that protect shorelines from wave action.

Sea Fan

Gorgonia ventalina

These delicate, fan-shaped corals filter plankton from the water and can grow up to 6 feet tall.

Pillar Coral

Dendrogyra cylindrus

This rare coral forms unique vertical columns that can reach heights of 10 feet in Caribbean waters.

Fire Coral

Millepora alcicornis

Not a true coral, this hydrozoan delivers a painful sting to deter predators and curious divers.

Threats to Coral Reefs

These fragile ecosystems face unprecedented challenges in our changing world

Climate Change

Rising ocean temperatures cause coral bleaching, where corals expel their symbiotic algae and turn white. Prolonged bleaching leads to coral death.

Ocean Acidification

Increased CO2 absorption makes oceans more acidic, reducing the availability of calcium carbonate that corals need to build their skeletons.

Overfishing

Removing key fish species disrupts the delicate balance of reef ecosystems, allowing algae to overgrow and smother corals.

Pollution

Runoff containing fertilizers, pesticides, and sewage causes algal blooms that block sunlight and deplete oxygen in reef waters.

Destructive Fishing

Practices like blast fishing and cyanide fishing physically destroy coral structures to capture fish for aquariums and food markets.

Coastal Development

Construction, dredging, and sedimentation from coastal projects smother corals and reduce water quality in nearby reef areas.

Save Our Reefs

Coral reefs worldwide are declining at an alarming rate. With your help, we can protect these vital ecosystems for future generations.
