Our Shared History

From ancient times to modern day, humans have always been connected to the ocean in profound ways.

Ancient Mariners

The earliest civilizations developed along coastlines, using the ocean for food, trade, and exploration. Polynesian navigators crossed vast distances using stars and wave patterns.

3000 BCE

Age of Exploration

Ocean voyages connected continents, leading to cultural exchanges, new trade routes, and scientific discoveries that changed human history forever.

1400-1600

Industrial Revolution

Massive changes in fishing technology and shipping transformed our relationship with the sea, increasing both dependence and impact on marine ecosystems.

1800s

Modern Oceanography

The 20th century brought new understanding of ocean systems and our dependence on them, revealing both their fragility and their critical role in Earth's climate.

1900s

Conservation Era

Today we recognize the need to protect marine environments while sustainably benefiting from ocean resources, balancing human needs with ecological health.

21st Century

How the Ocean Sustains Us

The ocean provides countless benefits that are essential to human survival and wellbeing.

Oxygen Production

Marine plants produce over 50% of Earth's oxygen through photosynthesis, with phytoplankton being the most significant contributors to our atmosphere.

Food Source

The ocean provides a primary protein source for over 3 billion people, with sustainable fisheries being crucial for global food security.

Climate Regulation

Oceans absorb about 30% of carbon dioxide and 90% of excess heat from human activities, acting as Earth's largest carbon sink and climate buffer.

Medical Discoveries

Marine organisms have provided breakthrough medicines, including cancer treatments, pain relievers, and anti-inflammatory compounds.

Economic Value

Ocean-based industries contribute over $1.5 trillion annually to the global economy through shipping, tourism, energy, and other sectors.

Mental Health

Proximity to water has been scientifically proven to reduce stress and anxiety while increasing feelings of happiness and wellbeing.
